Hot zones: urban and commuter communities in NJ face higher COVID-19 positivity rates than their rural neighbors

By Brenna Holland

December 17, Fort Lee, NJ—COVID-19 positivity rates continue to rise across New Jersey, with growing hot zones in urban and commuter counties like Middlesex, Bergen, Essex and Passaic.

As of Thursday, 4,255 new cases have been reported across the Garden State, many of them concentrated in these densely-populated neighborhoods, with residents traveling into and out-of New York each day.

Hospitalizations have remained relatively flat for over a week, with 3,635 hospital beds currently occupied. Of these hospitalized New Jersians, 715 are receiving intensive care, 480 are on ventilators and 461 were discharged on Thursday.

77 new, COVID-19-related deaths have been reported, pushing the state-wide death-toll since the beginning of the pandemic over the 18,000 mile-marker.

“These numbers will not magically return to zero because we’re about to provide our first vaccinations,” Governor Phil Murphy said in his most-recent COVID-19 briefing. “For us to reach the level of so-called ‘herd immunity,’ it will require 70% of New Jersey’s adults to be vaccinated.”
